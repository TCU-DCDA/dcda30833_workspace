Two Modes of the Sublime

Mathematical Sublime

Vastness that defeats our ability to grasp it as a whole

Examples: The starry sky · The ocean · Geological time · Infinite patterns

Dynamical Sublime

Power that exceeds our capacity to resist or control

Examples: Storms · Volcanoes · Waterfalls · Forces beyond human scale

The Key Insight

The sublime is not in the object
but in the experience of our own limits.

It's not that the ocean is sublime.
It's that confronting the ocean reveals something about us—
our smallness, our inability to comprehend totality.

Edmund Burke

"The passion caused by the great and sublime in nature... is Astonishment; and astonishment is that state of the soul, in which all its motions are suspended, with some degree of horror."

A Philosophical Enquiry into the Origin of Our Ideas of the Sublime and Beautiful (1757)

The Romantic Extension

For Romantic artists and writers, the sublime became central:
a way of valuing experiences that exceed rational control.

  • The value of being overwhelmed
  • Nature as a force beyond human intention
  • Art as a means of accessing experiences larger than the self

Artists: J.M.W. Turner · Caspar David Friedrich · William Wordsworth

Immanuel Kant

"The sublime is the feeling of the superiority of our own power of reason, as a supersensible faculty, over nature."

But here, the "nature" is the system itself—
the code, the constraint, the chance operation.

And the "superiority" is not mastery but recognition:
I designed this system, but it shows me things I could not have imagined.

The Mathematical Sublime in Code

I understand the parts,
but the whole exceeds my grasp.

When you run your code and see something you didn't expect—
something beautiful, strange, or disturbing—
you have encountered the computational sublime.

Why Discovery Over Expression

Expression assumes the artist knows in advance what they want to say.

Discovery acknowledges that the system has its own tendencies,
its own emergent behaviors, its own capacity to surprise.

This is not randomness for its own sake.
It is a stance: the willingness to be surprised.

The Pedagogical Reframe

Students often arrive expecting creative coding to be about control—
making the computer do exactly what they want.

The sublime offers a counter-narrative:
The most interesting creative experiences may come from loss of control,
from designing systems that exceed our predictions.

A Liberating Framework

Discovery-based art does not require a message.
It requires a willingness to design systems
and attend to what emerges.

This can be freeing for students who feel pressure to "have something to say."

The Ethical Dimension

The Technological Sublime

David Nye describes how Americans have historically experienced technology
(railroads, skyscrapers, atomic bombs) with a mix of awe and unease.

In the context of AI and generative systems, this ambivalence returns:

  • Wonder at what the system produces
  • Unease about authorship and control
  • Questions about what it means to "make" something

The Sublime as Framework

The sublime offers a way to process these feelings.

We can value the experience of encountering
something beyond our full comprehension
while remaining thoughtful about our relationship
to the systems we use.

Against the Cult of Control

Much of technology culture valorizes
mastery, optimization, and control.

The sublime offers a counter-value:
There is worth in being overwhelmed,
in failing to fully comprehend,
in designing systems that exceed us.

This Is Not Passivity

  • It takes skill to design a system that can surprise you
  • It takes judgment to recognize when emergence is interesting
  • It takes courage to let go of predetermined outcomes

Paul Klee

"An active line on a walk, moving freely, without goal."

Pedagogical Sketchbook (1925)

Discussion

In small groups (5 minutes):

  1. Have you ever experienced something like the sublime while making or viewing generative art? What was that experience like?
  2. How does the concept of the sublime challenge the idea that art is primarily about self-expression?
  3. What is the relationship between control and creativity? Can surrendering control be a creative act?

Reflection Prompt

In your notes or journal:

Describe a moment when a system you created showed you something you didn't expect. How did that feel? Did it change how you approached the work?

Key Takeaways

  • The sublime is the experience of encountering what exceeds our comprehension
  • In generative art, emergence can function as a computational sublime
  • Discovery-based art positions us in relation to forces larger than ourselves
  • Surrendering control is not weakness—it's a deliberate creative stance
  • The sublime offers a framework for engaging with AI and complex systems
